Techniques for data science

1. Statistical Learning
Learning from data as a probabilistic problem

$(X,y)$ random variable (unknown).
$(X_1, y_1), (X_2, y_2), \dots, (X_n, y_n)$ i.i.d. samples from $(X,y)$ (labelled data).

Exploratory data analysis and statistics
  • Visualization and plots.
  • Estimation of statistics of $X$ from $X_1, \dots, X_n$.
Statistical models and function approximation Goal: Find $h$ that maximizes $$P(h(X_i)=y_i: 1\leq i \leq n).$$
  • Linear regression
  • Polynomial regression
  • Logistic regression
  • Relationship with Maximum Likelihood Estimation and Least Squares Estimation

Techniques for data science

2. Geometric Learning
Learning from the shape of data.

$X$ a geometric space (unknown). $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$ sample points from $X$.
The idea is to understand geometric aspects of $X$ from $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n$.

Clustering
Connected components.
  • Partitioning and $k$-means
  • Distances and hierarchical clustering
  • Density-based methods
    • Dimensionality Reduction
    $p:X\to \mathbb{R}^n$ ($n$ 'small') such that $p(X)$ preserves properties of $X$.
  • PCA
  • Multidim. Scaling Proj.
  • UMAP
  • ISOMAP
  • Manifolds and local dim.
    • Topological Data Analysis
    Higher dimensional topological features (e.g. holes).
  • Geometric reconstructions from data
  • Persistent Homology
  • Geometric Anomalies
